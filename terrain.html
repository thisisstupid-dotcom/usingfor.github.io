03 / Knowing the terrain

I've already studied the market Optus competes in

Outside the capstone, I analysed two telcos for my digital marketing portfolio: an incumbent (Telstra) against a challenger (Aussie Broadband), mapping how their digital strategies differ.

The short version: incumbents win on scale and trust; challengers attack on authenticity. I worked this out using —

  • Chaffey's consumer decision journey — to trace how each brand meets customers at every stage.
  • The RACE planning model — Reach, Act, Convert, Engage — the same backbone behind the social content a brand team runs.
  • Brand community theory — to understand why the challenger's authenticity play earns loyalty an incumbent has to work harder for.

Why this matters for Optus: understanding how challengers come at an incumbent is intelligence a brand team can actually use to defend and grow.

I can also build a brand from zero

For a separate unit I helped create One Shot, a Gen-Z energy-and-electrolyte capsule, owning the brand experience: consumer-journey pain points, product and service fixes, and a refill program as the key point of difference. So I'm comfortable on both sides — defending an established brand and launching a new one.
