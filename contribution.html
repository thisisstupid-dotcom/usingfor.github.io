02 / My contribution

From idea to go-to-market

This is the part of the capstone I'm proudest of: my individual go-to-market chapter.

  1. I sequenced the launch instead of forcing it. Rather than open all three sides at once and hope everyone arrives together, I seeded one side first to create the value that pulls the others in.
  2. I built the B2B acquisition funnel. The partner organisations anchor the whole platform, so I mapped how we'd identify, approach, and convert them — stage by stage, not a vague "we'll do outreach."
  3. I modelled the unit economics. I modelled lifetime value against customer acquisition cost (LTV:CAC) so the strategy could be funded and defended, not just admired. A campaign that can't justify its spend doesn't survive contact with a real business.

What it taught me

Go-to-market isn't one decision, it's a sequence of them, and each one has to earn the next. I didn't come in knowing unit economics — I taught myself the model because the strategy didn't hold up without it. I'd rather learn the hard part than hand in something that looks good and falls over.
